American Rapper Mac Miller

Mac's Milestones and Achievements

  • 2010 - Releases his debut mixtape "K.I.D.S.," gaining recognition for his laid-back flow and catchy hooks.
  • 2011 - His debut studio album "Blue Slide Park" becomes the first independently distributed album to debut at number one on the Billboard 200 since 1995.
  • 2011 - Collaborates with Donald Glover (Childish Gambino) on the song "Donald Trump," which gains significant popularity and showcases his lyrical skills.
  • 2013 - Earns his first Grammy nomination for Best Rap Album with "Watching Movies with the Sound Off," created in collaboration with producer Pharrell Williams.
  • 2014 - Founds REMember Music, his own record label, to support and promote emerging artists.
  • 2014 - Releases his critically acclaimed mixtape "Faces," which explores themes of addiction, mental health, and introspection.
  • 2016 - Drops "The Divine Feminine," showcasing a more soulful and mature sound while receiving praise for its poetic exploration of love and relationships.
  • 2018 - Releases his fifth studio album "Swimming," which receives widespread acclaim for its introspective lyrics and unique blend of hip-hop and jazz influences.
  • 2018 - Tragically passes away at the age of 26, leaving behind a profound impact on the music industry and a devoted fan base mourning his loss.
  • 2020 - Posthumously wins the Best Rap Album Grammy for "Swimming," honoring his artistic contributions and further solidifying his legacy.
  • 2020 - His posthumous album "Circles" is released, receiving widespread acclaim and showcasing his musical evolution.
  • 2021 - His family launches The Mac Miller Fund, a charitable organization supporting young musicians and providing resources for addiction recovery.
